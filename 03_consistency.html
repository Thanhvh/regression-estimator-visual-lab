🔒 Consistency says nothing about your finite n: a consistent estimator can still be poor at the sample size you actually have, and an inconsistent one may be tolerable if its asymptotic bias is small. Consistency is a promise about the limit — which is why Lessons 1–2 still matter.
plim behaves well (Slutsky: plim of a function = function of the plim), unlike the expectation operator — which is why IV, GMM, MLE and dynamic-panel methods are justified by consistency, not unbiasedness. Cases B (endogeneity) and C (biased-but-consistent) are the gateway to IV / GMM later in the course.
